Yinghua Zhang Large Tech companies' success in the U.S. based on their large back ground invest and research, and complicated cooperation between many sub-sectors. They spend a lot money and training time on the new graduate STEM students to make them familiar with all these necessary aspects so they are able to contribute their merit to the employer. However, 17 months OPT extension is obviously not long enough for the employers to see the profit from their invest. After all the training and practice, when a STEM student just become a mature engineer who could really do some work, they have to leave their position for the expiring work authorization. Such a waste needs to be ended. A new 24 month OPT extension is a good sign to solve this problem.
