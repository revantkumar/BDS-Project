Minghe Li The improving and expanding training opportunities for F-1 non-immigrant students with STEM degrees and cap-gap relief for all eligible F-1 students can be very beneficial for both the F-1 student with STEM degrees and the United State of America as a whole. First of all, most F-1 student with STEM degree that found a job in US are very good at what they studies and can bring lots of reinforcement and improvement to the company they are in, which can potentially increase the economic environment of the whole country. The elites of these students can also have much better chance to apply for a work visa during this extension to stay in USA and continue their contribution to the this country.
