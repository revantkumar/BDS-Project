This a a great fix to our broken immigration system. We should all welcome this proposal. 

For the sake of the U.S. economy's recovery and long-term competitiveness, lawmakers should couple new policies to improve STEM training throughout the U.S. educational system with revisions to the antiquated rules that currently govern how many and which scientists and engineers from abroad are allowed to work in the United States. Reform initiatives that address the imbalance between the demands of the U.S. economy and the archaic constraints of the U.S. immigration system are an important component of overhauling the immigration system.
