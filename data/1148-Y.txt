The proposed rule, if made final, will not be detrimental to the native U.S. worker as has been addressed by some people. On the contrary, with such an extension the U.S. could be a more attractive destination for talents from all over the world and the potential benefits brought by this force is significant. This Butter-fly effects would correspondingly create even more opportunities in the job market of different levels. More importantly, foreign work force, either immigrants or non-immigrants are major contributors to the country's development, this is especially true in high-tech realm. Closing this door to F-1 students across the world would potentially affect the healthy and comprehensive operation mechanism of the high level education field as well as the high-tech field, however, it doesn't necessarily mean that the job opportunities of U.S. citizens will increase.
