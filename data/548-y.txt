Andrew Taylor If this rule is not passed, foreign STEM grads will take their skills and education to their home countries, costing America the opportunity for job-creating innovation. This new rule will help international STEM graduates to stay in the country for longer time and grow in the technical field and ultimately it will contribute towards the growth of U.S.A. This rule is very beneficial for retaining talented international students. There will be adequate time for students to devote for the country where they studied several years.. This rule will strengthen the relationships between America and other countries. That is, they can share more information, more resources and more opportunities.
