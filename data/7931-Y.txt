Anne Davidson International students are also helping to fill skills shortages at a time when the US share of the world's science and engineering graduates is declining. This will ensure continuous supply of young talented STEM graduates and promote growth of science and technology. This new rule will bring global perspectives into U.S. classrooms and research labs. Economists have argued that the technology those STEM workers helped create has accounted for nearly half of the nation's economic growth in the second half of the twentieth century. This rule is very beneficial for retaining talented international students.
