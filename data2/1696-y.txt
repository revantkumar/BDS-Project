Josh Wu In fact, OPT extension is a very economic way to keep talented people working in the US. Employers will do everything to keep good employees, rather than hiring other unqualified people and spend time and money on training again. If the employee's OPT is going to expire, the employer need to spend more money to support the employee to stay in the US through other legal approaches, for instance, applying working visa or green card, which is expensive and time-consuming. So both employer or employees would be benefited if the OPT can be extended longer.
