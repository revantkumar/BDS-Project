Yaqi Zhao After receiving education and gaining practical experience during OPT in U.S., these international students can be really productive and contribute to U.S. economy. This is especially true in the field of science & technology, where the highly skilled workers are most needed. 
Canceling the STEM OPT will make U.S. universities less attractive to international students. Tuition is one important economic source for U.S. universities. 
If fewer and fewer international students choose to work in U.S., the salary of domestic workers might increase temporarily, but in the price of hurting U.S. economy in the long time. The highly skilled international students go to the competitor country, and U.S. might face a lack of workers.
