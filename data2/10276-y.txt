Rujian Fu This rule is beneficial to both international students and US economy. Let's look at the statistics reported by Association of International Educators. Foreign students made a net contribution of $26.8 billion to the U.S. economy in the 2013-2014 academic year. Colleges benefit a lot from international tuition, which is way higher than in state tuition that most citizens paid. 

If OPT gets banned, international students are not coming to US for colleges. That is a big financial loss to educational organizations! Not to mention the financial loss in STEM industries who couldn't find enough STEM candidates to work for them.
