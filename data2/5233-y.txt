Harry Davies International students bring money, skills and jobs in USA. The proposal to reinstitute the STEM extension will provide valuable hands-on, educational experience in which STEM graduates gain real-world immersion into a chosen industry. This experiential learning will allow them to integrate knowledge and theory learned in the classroom with practical application and skills development in a professional setting. STEM students will allow employers the opportunity to accomplish bigger projects, achieve more innovation, and develop new products and technology, all of which will further fuel the U.S. economy. There will be adequate time for students to devote for the country where they studied several years.. This rule will strengthen the relationships between America and other countries. That is, they can share more information, more resources and more opportunities.
